# Review Paper Format Guide
### For AI Agents & Students — University Assignment Submission
**Version 1.0 | Generic Academic Domain | Based on Whiteboard Template + Real Paper Analysis**

---

## PURPOSE OF THIS GUIDE

This document defines the **exact format and structure** for writing a university-level review paper.
It serves two audiences:
- **AI Agents** — follow the strict rules to auto-generate compliant papers
- **Students** — use this as a step-by-step writing guide and self-check reference

> **Master Reference:** The Whiteboard Template is the primary authority.
> The real paper example is used for illustration only. Where they conflict, the Whiteboard Template wins.

---

## FONT & TYPOGRAPHY RULES

These are **STRICT** — agents must follow exactly.

| Element | Font | Size | Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paper Title | Times New Roman | 24pt | Bold, Centered |
| Author Name | Times New Roman | 12pt | Normal |
| Author Details (dept, university, email) | Times New Roman | 12pt | Normal |
| Section Headings | Times New Roman | 16–14pt | Bold |
| Body Text | Times New Roman | 12pt | Normal |
| Table Caption | Times New Roman | 9pt | Bold |
| Figure Caption | Times New Roman | 9pt | Normal |
| Abstract Label + Keywords Label | Times New Roman | 12pt | Bold |

> **Flexibility Note:** If Times New Roman is unavailable, use Arial or Calibri consistently throughout. Never mix fonts.

---

## SECTION ORDER — STRICT

The paper must follow this exact order. Do not rearrange sections.

```
1. Title
2. Author Block
3. Abstract
4. Keywords
5. I.   Introduction
6. II.  Objective
7. III. Literature Review
8. IV.  Comparison of Published Research Papers (Table)
9. V.   Conclusion
10. VI.  Future Scope
11.     References
```

> **Common Student Error:** Many students place Objective after Literature Review. It must come BEFORE.

---

## 1. TITLE

### Strict Rules
- Centered on the page
- Font size: **24pt, Bold**
- Use **Title Case** (capitalize all major words)
- Introduce acronym in parentheses on first use within the title if needed — e.g., `Virtual Reality (VR)`
- Do NOT use a subtitle or a colon in the title
- Keep it concise — ideally under 12 words

### Format
```
<Title of the Review Paper>
```

### Example
```
Computer Graphics Techniques for Virtual Reality (VR)
```

---

## 2. AUTHOR BLOCK

### Strict Rules
- Presented as a **3-column borderless table**
- One author per column (max 3 authors per row; add more rows if more authors)
- **No table borders** — invisible grid only
- Columns are equal width, center-aligned

### Each Column Must Contain (in this order):
```
Line 1: Full Name (First Last)
Line 2: Department Name
Line 3: University/College Name, City, State
Line 4: Email Address (hyperlinked, underlined)
```

### Flexible Rules
- Designation (e.g., "Assistant Professor") may be added between Name and Department for faculty authors
- Student authors typically omit designation

---

## 3. ABSTRACT

### Strict Rules
- Label format: `Abstract —` (bold label, followed by an **em-dash**, NOT a colon)
- The label is **inline** with the text — not a separate heading line
- Length: **12–15 lines** (approximately 200–300 words)
- Written in **third person**, **passive voice** preferred

### Required Content Structure (all 6 points must be covered):

| Point | What to Write |
|---|---|
| 1 | Brief introduction to the topic and its importance |
| 2 | The core challenge or problem being reviewed |
| 3 | Specific techniques/methods reviewed in the paper |
| 4 | Optimization strategies or approaches discussed |
| 5 | Key challenges identified across the reviewed literature |
| 6 | Closing statement — overall contribution and future direction |

---

## 4. KEYWORDS

### Strict Rules
- Label format: `Keywords —` (bold label, em-dash, same style as Abstract)
- Count: **5 to 7 keywords** strictly
- Format: Comma-separated, each keyword in **Title Case**
- Placement: Immediately after the abstract, before Section I
- No period at the end

---

## 5. SECTION I — INTRODUCTION

### Strict Rules
- Heading format: `I. Introduction` — Roman numeral, period, space, Title Case
- Must contain:
  1. Brief background on the topic (2–3 paragraphs)
  2. **A Flowchart** — labeled as `Figure 1. <Name of the Figure>`
  3. **A Block Diagram** — labeled as `Figure 2. <Name of the Figure>` (or next number)
- Figure captions go **below** the figure, font size 9pt

---

## 6. SECTION II — OBJECTIVE

### Strict Rules
- Heading format: `II. Objective`
- Length: **1 paragraph** (5–8 sentences)
- Written in third person
- Must clearly state:
  - What the paper aims to do
  - What techniques or methods are studied
  - What the expected outcome or contribution is

---

## 7. SECTION III — LITERATURE REVIEW

### Strict Rules
- Heading format: `III. Literature Review`
- **Exactly 30 paragraphs** — one paragraph per reviewed paper
- Each paragraph is **3–4 sentences** in this exact pattern:
  1. Sentence 1: What the paper proposes or investigates
  2. Sentence 2: The method or approach used
  3. Sentence 3: Key result or finding
  4. Sentence 4 (optional): Significance or implication
- Citation number in **square brackets** at the **end** of the paragraph: `[1]`, `[2]`... `[30]`
- Do NOT mention author names in the paragraph body
- Do NOT use bullet points — all prose paragraphs

### Paragraph Template
```
This [paper/research/study] [proposes/investigates/presents/introduces] [topic/method].
The [approach/technique] [does what]. [Key result/finding]. [Citation number].
```

---

## 8. SECTION IV — COMPARISON TABLE

### Strict Rules
- Heading format: `IV. Comparison of [N] Published Research Papers`
- Must begin with **one introductory paragraph** before the table
- Table caption format: `Table 1. <Descriptive Name>` — placed **above** the table, bold, 9pt
- Table contains exactly **5 rows** of data (5 selected papers from the 30)
- **7 columns required:**

| Column | Header | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | S.No | Serial number 1–5 |
| 2 | Title of the RP | Full or shortened paper title |
| 3 | Author Name | Last et al. (Year) format |
| 4 | Year | Publication year |
| 5 | Objective | 1 sentence |
| 6 | Methodology | 1 sentence |
| 7 | Conclusion/Result | 1 sentence |

---

## 9. SECTION V — CONCLUSION

### Strict Rules
- Heading format: `V. Conclusion`
- Length: **3 paragraphs**
- Structure:

| Paragraph | Content |
|---|---|
| 1 | Summary of techniques and methods reviewed |
| 2 | Remaining challenges and limitations identified |
| 3 | Forward-looking closing statement |

---

## 10. SECTION VI — FUTURE SCOPE

### Strict Rules
- Heading format: `VI. Future Scope`
- Length: **1 paragraph** (8–12 sentences, ~150 words)
- Must mention: emerging technologies, application domains, hardware improvements, open problems

---

## 11. REFERENCES

### Strict Rules
- Label: `References` — bold, no section number, no Roman numeral
- Style: **APA format**
- Numbered in **square brackets**: `[1]`, `[2]`... `[30]`
- Exactly **30 references** corresponding to the 30 literature review paragraphs

### APA Format Templates

**Journal Article:**
```
[N] Last, F. I., & Last, F. I. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, Volume(Issue), pages.
```

**Conference Paper:**
```
[N] Last, F. I., & Last, F. I. (Year). Title of paper. In Proceedings of Conference Name (pp. pages).
```

## AI AGENT INSTRUCTIONS

```
INPUT: Topic + List of 30 papers

STEP 1 → Generate Title (24pt, bold, centered, Title Case, <12 words)
STEP 2 → Generate Author Block (3-col borderless table)
STEP 3 → Generate Abstract (inline bold label + em-dash, 6-point structure)
STEP 4 → Generate Keywords (bold label + em-dash, 5–7 items)
STEP 5 → Generate Section I: Introduction (background + Flowchart + Block Diagram)
STEP 6 → Generate Section II: Objective (1 paragraph, 3rd person)
STEP 7 → Generate Section III: Literature Review (30 paragraphs, [citation] at end)
STEP 8 → Generate Section IV: Comparison Table (intro + 7-column table, 5 rows)
STEP 9 → Generate Section V: Conclusion (3 paragraphs)
STEP 10 → Generate Section VI: Future Scope (1 paragraph, ~150 words)
STEP 11 → Generate References (APA format, [1]–[30])
```

### Strict Rules for Agents
- NEVER skip a section
- NEVER reorder sections
- ALWAYS use Roman numerals for section numbers (I, II, III...)
- ALWAYS end each literature paragraph with `[N]` citation
- ALWAYS include exactly 7 columns in the comparison table
- NEVER use bullet points in the literature review section
- NEVER exceed 7 keywords
